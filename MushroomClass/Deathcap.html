Death Cap

Amanita phalloides

Description

Amanita phalloides, more commonly as the death cap mushroom is a deadly and toxic mushroom originating from Europe but was later introduced into many different parts of the world since the late 20th century. These mushrooms appear during summer and autumn.

The death cap resembles several edible species such as: Ceasar's murshroom and the straw mushroom which are commonly consumed by humans, increasing the risk of accidental consumption and poisoning.

Toxins

This mushroom contains amatoxins, a type of toxin that are resistant toward changes when exposed to heat or cold, keeping their effects where frozen or cooked. As little as half of the mushroom contains toxin that is enough to kill and adult human, and is responsible for 90% of mushroom-related fatalities worldwide, being involved in the majority of mushroom poisoning cases.

Classification: Toxic

Family: Amanita

Genus: Amanita

Edible? No
