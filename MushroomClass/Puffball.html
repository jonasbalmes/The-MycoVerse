Giant Puffball

Calvatia gigantea

Description

Calvatia gigantea, more commonly known as the Giant puffball is a type of Edible mushroom that can be found in meadows, fields, and deciduous forests during late summer and autumn and can be found within temperate areas of the world.

The interior of an immature puffball is white, while that of a mature specimen is greenish brown. This mushroom develops within a few weeks and soon begins to decompose and rot, at which point it becomes dangerous to consume. Unlike most mushrooms, all the spores are develop inside the mushroom with mature specimens containing several trillions.

Culinary use

This mushroom is edible when young, and so do other puffballs, however they may cause digestive issues if the spores have begun to form as indicated by the yellowish or greenish-brown flesh instead of being pure white, and should be discarded immediately. Some people may also experience laxative effects when consuming this species.

Classification: Edible

Family: Agaricales

Genus: Calvatia

Edible? Yes, if young
