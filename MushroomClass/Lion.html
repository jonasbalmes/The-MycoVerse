Lion's Mane Mushroom

Hypomyces lactifluorum

Description

Hypomyces lactifluorum, more commonly known as the Lion's Mane Mushroom is a type of medicinal mushroom and is a species of tooth fungus. It tends to grow in a single clump with dangling spines longer than one centimetre.

This type of mushroom is native to North America and Eurasia, and can be found frowing during late summer and autumn on hardwoods, particularly American beech and maple. It is typically considered as a species that feeds on dead matter, mostly feeding on dead trees, although it could also sometimes be found on living trees, usually in association with a wound.

Medicinal use

The Lion's Mane is edible and is commonly used in traditional Chinese medicine. It is generally safe to eat although, its medicinal effectiveness, safety in supplements, and active compounds are still unproven.

Classification: Medicinal

Family: Hericiaceae

Genus: Hericium

Edible? Yes
